# Alexandra Cárdenas
## A live coding presentation by Isaac P
- Alexandra is a composer/ live coder from Bogota, Colombia who has been live coding in Super Collider and Tidal cycles after being exposed to the ideas of live coding at National Center for the Arts in Mexico City
- What really stood out to me about Alexandra was her use of sample manipulation within tidal cycles to create huge and interesting walls of sound
- This is a quick example of some of the sample manipulation and her style of live coding 
[![Watch the video](https://img.youtube.com/vi/gxZzeCScn2U/hqdefault.jpg)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gxZzeCScn2U)

## Preferred Platforms
- Alexandra having started on super collider finds that the most interesting to her and in her own words describes it as "the most mind-blowing environment for creating audio in real time"
- Her use of tidal cycles is directly tied to her supercollider use and she uses tidal cycles to directly trigger sounds from super collider
- Shes uses both in tandem so simple lines of code in tidal cycles trigger complex patterns and sound design that is in Super collider which are the best tools for Alexandra to play generative algorithmic music live

## Artists Vision and Process
- Music has always been patterns that can be modified in real time to Cárdenas. Through live coding she has been able to create music in a way that more is in line with her thinking opposed to traditional daws that can make production feel plane and not finished. 
- Her live coding process has allowed her to continually evolve and change her productions and live performances to be a living breathing thing opposed to a static piece of art that is left and forgotten.

## Algorithims in Production
- Cárdenas is using Stochastic processes constantly throughout her process and music. Its involved in her sounds and her form and timing of the whole performance and piece. 
- I had never heard of the Stochastic process before this and was interested in how this actually was used in her pieces
- The Stochastis Process is a way to mathimatically describe a change over time with randomness essentially but there is a pattern to it to a degree. Such as the weather of today affects the weather of tomorrow but the weather of tomorrow can't always be predicted.
- This use of the Stochastis Process leads to complex and evolving pieces that have a pattern but there is constant change and randomness to help tickle your ear in the right ways

## In Conclusion
- Alexandra Cárdenas is a fascinating and skilled live coder who creates extremely ear catching and interesting ambient pieces but also uses these skills to create beats that reverbertate through your whole body and is a live coder/ artist to keep your eye on and pay attention to.
